1. Get Audio File of Subject

  1. Find a YouTube video of Professor Ed Kaplan speaking (preferably alone and with minimal background noise).
  2. Go to Tuberipper.com, paste the video URL, click Go, and select Extract Audio MP3.
  3. Download the MP3 file.

2. Trim Audio Clips (10 seconds each)

  1. Go to audiotrimmer.com and upload your MP3.
  2. Use the slider to trim a 10-second clip. Click Crop and download it.
  3. Click Go Back and repeat to get 2â€“3 clips.

3. Clone Voice in ElevenLabs

  1. Log into ElevenLabs and click Go to App.
  2. Click Voices on the left, then Create or Clone a Voice.
  3. Choose Instant Voice Clone and upload your audio clips.
  4. Name the voice, choose language, add a short description, and click Save Voice.

4. Create the Conversational Agent

  1. From the ElevenLabs App, click Conversational AI > Agents > + New Agent.
  2. Select Blank Template and click Create Agent.
  3. Fill in the Agent name, language, and first message.
  4. For the system prompt, open ChatGPT and type:
    5. 
"Write a system prompt for an AI Teaching Assistant for [class] that acts like [describe person]. 
The class is about [describe class]. Make sure the TA guides students through problems without giving full answers. 
Use the RAG for homework solutions but do not give solutions directly. The TA should be [describe personality traits]."
  5. Paste the generated system prompt into ElevenLabs under System Prompt.
  6. Upload PDFs of homework assignments and solutions to the Knowledge Base. Check Use RAG.
  7. Choose your large language model (the underlying AI) under LLM.
  8. Under the Voice tab, choose the cloned voice.
  9. Under the Advanced tab, set Max Conversation Duration.
  10. Under Widget, upload an avatar image (optional).
  11. Click Save, then click Test AI Agent to try it out!

5. Create Web App to Chat with AI TA

This is a bit more advanced, and most likely we will not be able to do this in class, but you can do it on your own later. You may experience some headaches with this part, but that will go away as the AI gets smarter over time.
  1. Go to Claude (https://claude.ai/) and type:
    2. 
"App maker, app maker, make me an app. I want a React app. I have PDFs of homework assignments. The filenames are pset1.pdf, pset 2.pdf, and pset3.pdf.
I want the app to let me choose one of the PDFs (side menu) and then it shows the PDF in the middle in a nice PDF viewer. 
Then I have an ElevenLabs conversational agent widget. I will paste this widget code. 
Show me where to paste it with a comment like ""ElevenLabs widget code goes here". 

I will run this in VS Code. 
Tell me how to do it step-by-step."
  2. Follow the instructions provided by Claude to build the app in VS Code.
  3. Paste the widget code into the placeholder comment in the React component.

That's it! You've created your own AI Teaching Assistant using a cloned voice and course material. Test it, refine it, and deploy it to help guide students in the modern age of education.
